Our Story

👨‍🍳JOE MARTINEZ

Who We Are

Joe Martinez grew up watching his grandfather, Ernesto, smoke brisket on an old oil-drum pit every Sunday in San Antonio. There was no recipe card — just feel, instinct, and the understanding that the fire does the work if you set it up right. Joe learned by standing next to him for twenty years.

When Joe moved to Austin in 2007, he brought Ernesto's methods with him. He smoked for neighbors, then for friends of neighbors, then for people who had heard about it from friends of neighbors. By 2009, demand had outgrown his driveway and his patience for saying "next week." Joe's Barbecue opened on Pitmaster Drive that October with a two-ton offset smoker, six picnic tables, and a handwritten menu on a chalkboard.

Sixteen years later, the chalkboard is still up. The picnic tables got replaced twice. The smoker got a younger sibling. Everything else — the wood, the rub, the patience, the people — stayed exactly the same. Joe still shows up before 5am to load the pit. He doesn't trust anyone else to start the fire.

We're not a franchise. We're not a concept. We're a family that figured out how to feed a neighborhood, and we've been doing it ever since.

The Wood Matters

We use Texas post oak, pecan, and mesquite — never liquid smoke, never shortcuts. The wood is half the flavor.

02
Community First

We sponsor the little league team. We feed the fire station. We host Sunday Funday because the neighborhood deserves a place to just be together.

03
Never Rush It

Brisket takes 14 hours. Ribs take 6. Pulled pork takes 10. We sell out when we sell out. We don't make more just to have more.
